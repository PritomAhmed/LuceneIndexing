Design Week Real Projects Design Week Design Week Final Report is available   Design Week Final Report ( 77.4 MB)   “Real Projects” Design Week is a practitioner-led week-long charette on a service-learning project that engages students of different levels & disciplines, faculty, practitioners and local community in a studio-based learning environment for a common goal, that is, a high-impact design and planning educational experience. The Department of Landscape Architecture and Urban Planning, and the Real Projects Initiative of the College of Architecture are co-sponsoring the event. "Real Projects" Design Week will start January 14 at 8am in Geren Auditorium and continue until January 18, 2013 with events happening between 8:30am and 5:30pm each day. Presentations and awards will take place on Friday, January 18. Participants include faculty, local community reviewers and students from BLA, BSURPN, MLA, MLPD, MUP and ARCH. The firm Design Workshop will lead this event. Design Workshop was founded in 1969 by two North Carolina State University professors, Don Ensign and Joe Porter. Design Workshop has 12 principals and 77 employees in six cities: Austin, Aspen, Asheville, Denver, Salt Lake City and Tahoe. Design Workshop has led "Design Week" in 12 different universities between 2003 and 2012. The design problem for Design Week is a "Real Project." The City of College Station Planning & Development Services, Director Bob Cowell, will announce the project at the opening event. Daily Schedule Day 1: Introducing the project Day 2: Drawing from the shoulder Day 3: Drawing from the elbow Day 4: Drawing from the wrist Day 5: Presentations and awards A more detailed schedule is now available. Why Design Week? To excite students To provide a studio-like (or firm-like) experience To stimulate pedagogical ideas To experiment vertical studio To advocate pluridisciplinary (interdisciplinary) collaboratory To kick off the new semester Expected Learning Outcomes Students work on a real project with a service-learning mission Students engage in an interdisciplinary teamwork Students learn real world issues from various stakeholders Students interact with the top-notch practitioners directly Students sharpen communication skills verbally, graphically and in writing For more information, contact Ming-Han Li.
